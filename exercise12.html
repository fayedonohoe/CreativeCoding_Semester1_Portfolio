Assignment - Wrapping Paper

Creating a Pattern using P5.js that would be used to wrap a chocolate bar
December 2020

Parabolic Hyperbola Turned Wrapping Paper

Our assignment was to create a pattern, using P5.js, that would be printed and used to wrap a bar of chocolate.
During the research phase of this project, several design concepts were considered. I came across the use of parabolas, or "saddle roofing" in architecture. This involves the use of flat or straight lines, laid out in a way that creates the illusion of a curve.
I'm no stranger to this concept. Any of my secondary school teachers could attest to my work being covered in parabolic scribbles, so it seemed fitting I should try recreating this with code.

The Design
What makes this concept to fascinating, is how deceptively simple it is. It's just lines. Straight lines, and 150 lines of code.
After attempting and failing to apply coordinate geometry to the project, I decided to keep it simple, and keep it running.
The first step was to create a single design. This was achieved by creating a loop for each quadrant to draw the lines.
Next, was to add user interactivity for manipulation.
The number of lines per quadrant is determined by mapping the users mouseX position, and the scaling by mapping mouseY.

The Pattern
To create the pattern, a simple loop was iterated through. Due to the nature of this design, this could be used to bring each structure together perfectly. Scaling was applied to achieve different versions of the same pattern.

When the scaling is small and the number of lines per quadrant is high, it appears as though the background is gold and green, with black circles drawn over it.
When the scaling is larger, the lines of the quadrants intersect each other to display a completely different visual.

The Aestetic
As shown in the Invision Board linked below, different colour combinations were trialed until the green and gold was settled on.
The stroke weight of the lines were also altered. This, combined with the overlapping structures, give more depth to the canvas.
